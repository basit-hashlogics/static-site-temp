As of 2022, there are about 1.85 million apps in the iOS App Store and 2.56 million in the Google Play Store<\/a>. Many of these apps are available on both iOS and Android, while a smaller number are also accessible on less common operating systems like KaiOS and HarmonyOS.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

These applications have become integral to modern life. They provide access to extensive music libraries, find connections, organize schedules and documents, and enable cashless transactions.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The market for these applications is vast, with over six billion smartphone users globally, and approximately five billion of them have internet access. Some of the most widely used apps, like Facebook and YouTube, have larger user bases than the population of China.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

In this blog, we explore the differences between cross-platform app development and native app development. We outline the pros and cons of each approach to help you decide which one is best suited for your next mobile app project.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Cross-Platform Application vs Native Mobile App <\/strong><\/h2>\n\n\n\n

A cross-platform mobile application is developed from a single source code, enabling it to reach various operating systems through a single development project. These apps are built on cross-platform frameworks, which use a single API to interface with platform-specific SDKs for iOS or Android.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

On the other hand, Native mobile apps are created specifically for either Android or iOS devices. Developers use a programming language specific to the targeted operating system for coding these applications. <\/p>\n\n\n\n
